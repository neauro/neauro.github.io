May Garden/Growing Update: Radishes, Blueberries, Tillandsia, Succulents, & Etc.

May 19th, 2013

Garden stuff has been progressing without me writing much about it because recently I am lucky if I can manage to wake up early enough or stay up long enough at home to water anything, much less take photos of it and muse upon its growth. Suffice to say that at the start of spring I went crazy over some on-sale seeds and attempted to plant some in seed starter pots.

march pots

Dragon’s Tongue Wax Beans in particular looked super cool and exciting and got off to a great start indoors…

lil things

…but when I transplanted them outside, they didn’t last two weeks. Maybe I didn’t harden them off enough? Maybe, like swiss chard, they’re too delicate to withstand transplanting? When I transplanted them I also planted some new beans, and these happen to be doing really well. Unlike the indoor starts, they have very deep green leaves and thick stems.

transplants in the back, new beans in the front

My other surprise is the success of my radishes, which I planted outdoors in March alongside some arugula and swiss chard. Look, they’re doing it! They’re really growing!

radishes

They’re probably the most attractive-looking edible thing I’ve grown this far, especially since the spinach and swiss chard I started growing before last winter tasted pretty mediocre. My mistake was not staggering out their sowing, however, so I pretty much just have this batch and that’s it for now.

Speaking of the spinach, as soon as the heat started getting up to around 70 degrees Fahrenheit, they started sending up flowers. The plants rise up over my knee now, and their stalks are as thick as my fingers, and it’s hard to believe that they used to be just little seeds.

biiig spinaches

My lime tree has really perked up now that the weather has gotten better, though has still suffered from leaves that are pale green and strangely droopy and bendy. They feel almost elastic. Some internet research has uncovered that I, in my passion to not drown it, I may be under watering it. Since being given a couple thorough soaks, the leaves indeed seem a little stiffer and greener. I’ve also repotted it into something larger and more sturdy than its initial black pot. No flowers or fruit yet, probably because it’s been stressed and dehydrated for the better part of its life with me.



Speaking of stressed and dehydrated, before the winter started Andou and I received a very impressive-looking 30 year old jade tree…

jade tree on the sunniest part of our house, the laundry room

I was very concerned about killing it, especially since, despite my diligent watering, its leaves began to fall off in gooey yellow curls. Some internet research has uncovered that I, in my passion to not kill it, I was probably over watering it. I’ve since then toned down and the tree seems okay on its new perch over the dryer. One branch even bloomed little white blossoms! Whoa!

My air plants from last year are doing well. In the picture below, the top plant had one pup that is now fairly large, and the bottom actually had two pups, so it’s getting to be a handful.

spot the pups!

It’s been about a year since I got them at the UW Street Fair, and when the fair rolled around again this weekend I was excited to pick up more! Four more, in fact. I was excited to get ones with weirder tentacle shapes, and then also picked up two teensy blooming ones, one of which already looks like it’s growing a pup. The two little ones are already a year old and were blooming already, so it makes me a little bewildered about why my plants from last year are not blooming. Maybe I should invest in some fertilizer.

spot the pup!

Speaking of plants retrieved at fairs, my sedums are doing okay. The iceplant-looking one is faring the best of all of them. I’ve moved three of them to a little pot I bought at Daiso and tried to give them succulent soil to see if they would do any better than in normal potting soil, but thus far I don’t observe any difference.

sedums in a long pot

Speaking of succulent soil, the reason why I had some is because I got another succulent! I saw it on sale at the hardware store and couldn’t resist. Donkey/burro tail plants are so neat-looking. Some of the little bean-leaves had detached and fallen between into the dirt inside the pot, so I took them out and placed them in their own pots to see if they might grow into new plants.

burro tail on the dryer

And that’s (almost) all of what I have growing.
