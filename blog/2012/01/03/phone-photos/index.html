Phone Photos

Jan 3rd, 2012

university street station, and part of alex’s sleeve

I haven’t been taking many pictures recently. I tend to go on and off with photography in general (though associating my taking pictures with “photography” and its professional air still makes me wince), but I can tie my decrease in photo-taking activity to some specific things this time, namely:
  1. The purse that I am currently using for everything, a shoulder-bag Haiku that’s lightweight and perfect for notebooks but bulges with a D40 in it;
  2. My lack of personal computer with which to edit raws – my main computer broke after coming back from San Francisco, and I’ve been subsisting off Andou’s desktop and my work computer ever since.
As a result, I’ve been spending most of my time tinkering around with my iPod for photo-taking purposes. The picture quality, compared to my beloved 35mm lens, is obscene, but challenging. How do I get a good picture despite the gross noise and relative inability to get close to a subject?

I’ve been cheating with Instagram, which I initially despised as hipster filter city, but now enjoy, mainly because
  1. I’m over the arguments of what constitutes “real” photography, and
  2. “It’s just what it is – pictures from your phone.” Nothing more, nothing less. Thus was the revelation of Nicole, my old roommate.
The filters aren’t perfect (who would ever use Kelvin? I mean, really) and the faux tilt-shift filter can also look pretty fauxlty (ohoho). But I like my 24/7 shiba inu photo stream and the silent camaraderie of people liking your photos, somehow more earnest and without the nuance of self-interest on Tumblr. Plus, the constraints are a fun challenge.

how do i take a picture that will not suck?
