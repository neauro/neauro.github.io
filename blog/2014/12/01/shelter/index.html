Game: Shelter

Dec 1st, 2014

(via)

I can’t really help being the type of person who would be totally satisfied for hours finding stuff to dig up and feed to digital creatures. That you get to do this in Shelter while waddling around as a squat creature, followed by five other smaller squat creatures, is an unexpectedly satisfying plus.

In Shelter, you play the role of a mother badger tasked with foraging and protecting five little badger babbus. Shelter is So Pretty, though the lack of contrast was occasionally hard on my eyes, and confused my gameplay. For example, since you are cued to hungry babbus by looking at which one is less saturated than the others, I think I ended up overfeeding the babbu with a pattern that consistently made it look pale/hungry, even when it wasn’t.

[spoilers ahead]

I was determined to bring all my babbus through the game, but unfortunately lost one to the river, one to fire, and one to the hawk at the end. I wasn’t too surprised by the game’s bitterwsweet ending, but wish it had been performed differently — for one thing, I kept getting lost running back and forth between those final grasses — would it have been possible to do something like desaturate the direction from which you’d come, to make the forest endgame more obvious and maybe even ~paradisical~?

so paradise

It was also a shame to be picked off “against my will” at the end — thinking about it now, it would have been cool to maybe hear a babbu shriek, and then look back to see that a hawk is struggling to carry it away. By the end of the game, the babbus are pretty heavy after all — maybe capable of giving a good thrash — and at that point you could make the choice to go back and try to fight the bird off, at which point it gives you a couple good swipes and then lifts you away to continue the circle of life.

good luck my sweets

I’m really looking forward to Shelter 2, mostly because you’ll be able to run without fear of leaving babbus behind to fend for themselves. Also, those little mewling noises in the trailer.
