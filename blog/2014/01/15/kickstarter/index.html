Kickstarter and Completion

Jan 15th, 2014

a screenshot of my kickstarter profile

After reading of a case in which a Kickstarter backer scammed dozens of artists, one phrase stuck out to me. The phrase isn’t in that link, and unfortunately, I can’t find it in any others, but it goes something like: [The scammer] doesn’t give them any money, and gets to have all these projects shown on his profile.

It made me realize Kickstarter’s profiles worth looking at as a thing that people can actually be proud of. That appeal to a persons’s sense of progress, and the desire to gather things. The little pie chat, especially, could be an example of endowed progress. Even if there’s nothing necessarily telling you, “Go fill in your pie chart!” the fact that it’s there, and it’s incomplete…

Well, I feel compelled to fill it up. But I’m also the type of person that restarted my Metroid Prime save file when I realized I missed scanning the first boss.

Kickstarter also offers some encouragement when you click the empty slice of your pie chart.

“Let’s change that!”

It’s pretty cute that the slices are all (presumably) different colors, and I’m really tempted to fill them in with $1 pledges to relevant projects. I wonder how Kickstarter encourages users to back more projects if they have filled in all their slices?
