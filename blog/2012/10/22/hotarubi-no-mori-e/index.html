Hotarubi No Mori E

Oct 22nd, 2012

One of my favorite manga short stories, Hotarubi no Mori e by Yuki Midorikawa, was just made into an anime film! I cried after reading the story, and I cried after the animated film ended too. One would think that already knowing the ending would have removed the emotion from the film, but I guess not.

tears everywhere, seriously (source)

[spoilers ahead]

I haven’t read much manga recently, but what stories I’ve read from Midorikawa still remain glowing in my memory. She always takes a single, strange premise – a criminal investigator that can make people do as he says, a boy who inherits his grandmother’s “book of names” of spirits – and uses story to cast light into the unique possibilities given by that premise, really stretches and shines it out to its full potential.

Hotarubi no Mori e in particular is my favorite of hers, because it’s so wonderfully concise, whole, and focused. A human and a spirit meet and become friends, despite a peculiar limitation: if the spirit ever encounters human touch, he will vanish from the world.

Significant events and points of growth in the characters’ relationship revolve around the problem of contact. When the two first meet, they carry a stick between them to hold hands. One day, when the human falls from a tree, the spirit races to catch her – before pulling back at the last moment to let her crash. Afterward, she weeps – “Never touch me, ever!” – and those different facets of the premise sparkle to light. You understand it without the burden of over-narration; you understand simply, just as it happens. He can’t protect her. He can’t even comfort her. You watch her sob and sob and the spirit sits close by, face hidden by his mask.

When people say that they just had to keep reading/watching/consuming because they want to see what will happen – this story really dazed me with that desperation. The conflict seems so impossible – you know what’s going to happen, what must happen – but does it? Is there any other way that this story could end?

Do yourself a favor and find out! :)
