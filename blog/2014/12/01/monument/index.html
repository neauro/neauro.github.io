Game: Monument Valley

Dec 1st, 2014

a screenshot of Monument Valley

I played Monument Valley carefully, slowly, with headphones, trying to savor it. It’s tough with puzzle games, because if something takes you a little more effort than your flow state allows, your vision narrows, and it’s tough to think about anything except stuff like HOW DO I GET THROUGH THAT DOOR AUAUUGHHH

In Monument Valley, you lead (are?) Ida, a “silent princess,” through a geometrically implausible landscape. The game has very nice UI built in to facilitate you taking screenshots, which is a good feature for getting the word out. It’s fairly innocuous, since you do start to feel like you want to share where you’ve been in the game, even if it isn’t the sort of thing used for making unique creations.

here’s another screenshot, because i took a lot of them and consider them all gorg

The screenshot feature has the added benefit of guiding your player into how they should feel about the game too. This scene is pretty. Appreciate it! Emails sent with screenshots are loaded with the message “Wish you were here,” which is pretty cute.

My favorite bits (with spoilers):

  • The title card “flavors,” especially IN WHICH A THRONE LIES VACANT and IN WHICH THERE IS NOBODY LEFT TO FORGIVE US. These are the game’s last-subtle aspects, and though I can’t say with any specificity what they refer to (and would have preferred if I could), I like the poetry of them, and their ominousness.
  • Meeting your Yellow Block Friend. I loved how Ida would touch them when they were standing together. And I also loved watching them sink under the ocean, only to return later during the descent — so good! Their plodding after you until totally overtaken by watery vectors was pleasantly heartbreaking.
  • The whole “music box” level, as well as the levels in general where you start without knowing where Ida is. It makes the geometry of the game — and Ida’s navigation through it — a little more “impossible.”
