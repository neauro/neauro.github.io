Game: Kisima Inŋitchuŋa (Never Alone)

Nov 24th, 2014

(via)

The trailer was gorgeous and I’m a sucker for both folklore and foxes, so I played Never Alone this weekend. I started on my own and, after getting trapped in an area where character AI wasn’t quite cutting it (and kept dropping me into an icy demise), I set up a controller so I could play with a human. (It’s pretty easy, by the way!)

It turned out to be fortunate trouble because the game is much more fun with someone else, though I admit that this could also be because I rarely play multi-player games nowadays and hugely prefer them to be cooperative when I do. The atmosphere and Iñupiaq narration were fantastic, and I loved the subtlety of certain details, such as the little halo of light that emerged from the fox’s head whenever a spirit was near. Certain things didn’t quite make sense — why do the spirits of the aurora fly away with you? If it’s because you’re doing something “bad,” what is that bad thing you’re doing? — but it helps contributes to the surrealism that surrounds old stories.

I only wish the game was longer than the three hours it took us to complete it. Hopefully it’s successful enough to warrant the creation of further chapters, and inspires others to find new ways to share their cultural stories as well. I know I’d love to see a game with the Filipino pantheon, despite (or even because) of its incredible diversity and tension with Christianity.
