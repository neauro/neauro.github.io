The Women of Avacyn Restored

Apr 28th, 2012

I’ve been kind of annoyed with the women that are featured on Magic’s creatures before; in general, male characters have a ton of diversity (with representations as Rakish Heir, Thraben Doomsayer, Disciple of Griselbrand), whereas women tend to be like…Crossway Vampire, or even Morkrut Banshee, with only half a face to speak of but wearing a corset which features her perky boobs.

Imagery like this says: in Magic World, men can be a variety of things, they can be young or old or fat or wrinkled, but if women are there, she better be supple and breastful, like, maybe something that a guy can look at while his opponent is thinking out their turn.

Part of the reason why I started getting into Magic during the Innistrad block is because I loved the women werewolves and the fact they could become enormous unsexualized female creatures.  The release of Avacyn Restored, the final set of Innistrad, came with a ton of other fun female(-assigned?) creatures, like…


Farbog Explorer (source)

Farbog Explorer: Look at you.  Look at your hat.  You are awesome.


Sigarda, Host of Herons (source)
Sigarda, Host of Herons: who shows more variety to angelic armor than what’s shown on other angels like Herald of War the Angel token, who have that odd cleavage-bearing armor that most fantasy characters have.  In general, her femininity is also less pronounced than other Angel creatures.

Bruna, Light of Alabaster (source)
Speaking of angels, there’s Bruna, Light of Alabaster, who (despite potentially flashing everyone that she flies over), is a darker-skinned angel, which is pretty neat.

Cathedral Sanctifier (source)
Speaking of darker-skinned characters, Cathedral Sanctifier is also pretty neat – though her skin still strikes me as pretty light, which I mention still due to the privilege that lighter-skinned PoC have over darker-skinned PoC, as the movie Dark Girls points out in clarity.  Other cool priest-y creatures: Holy Justiciar, Devout Chaplain.

Thraben Valiant (source)
Thraben Valiant: Awesome armor, awesome impossibly-curved whip-thing, awesome hat.

Riders of Gavony (source)
Riders of Gavony: It’s a female human knight!  Go go go!

Kruin Striker (source)
Kruin Striker: I guess she wants to kill the wolfir, which is a little sad for me story-wise, since I like them…but I also appreciate that this provides more cool “women who have agendas!” flavor.  She also helped me in most of my pre-release matches, so, totally legit.

Elgaud Shieldmate (source)
Elgaud Shieldmate: So cool.  I only regret being unable to play her with Latch Seeker during pre-release.

Gryff Vanguard (source)
Gryff Vanguard: Another sweet human knight, plus she’s riding a cool heron-gryphon thing, so.

Champion of Lambholt (source)
Champion of Lambholt: Again, cool for adding to the variety of looks/things in Innistrad, and also for potentially overcoming the multitude of spirit tokens in the metagame, haha.


Lingering “Oh Well :/” Things:

  • The majority of creatures in Innistrad are still white.  I mean, I guess the entire set is inspired from European fantasies, and traditional Europe is white-skinned, but I just find it mind-boggling that it’s easier to imagine angels and demons living in a fictional world than it is to imagine darker-skinned humans.  It’s also frustrating to continually see high fantasy scenarios the realm of mostly-white characters.  Would the world-building really suffer for having more PoC there?  Would people get up in arms about it?  If so, consider that players would be arguing that they want their fictional worlds to be mostly white, and then realize what this means for semi-subconscious racism.
  • Even though there are more women and more types of women filling different roles in Innistrad, I would consider them all still conventionally beautiful in their thinness and youth.  Contrast, again, with the male characters: Diregraf Escort, Silverblade Paladin, and Timberland Guide (all of whom I consider middle-aged-looking), Nightshade Peddler (not conventionally beautiful, period), Alchemist’s Apprentice (which just makes me realize that I haven’t yet noticed a close-up of a female character that just shows her face, you know, minus boobs or identifying skirt), Nephalia Smuggler (who is clearly not thin)…and so on.
  • Both of the wolfir cards (Wolfir Silverheart and Wolfir Avenger) look pretty masculine to me, though CML thinks they look pretty androgynous.  The wolfir is what happened when Avacyn repressed the werewolf curse in certain humans, so given the density of female werewolves, I was a little disappointed there weren’t more female-looking wolfir…but in my mind perhaps they chose to stay as werewolves and live far away and free :)
  • The card Triumph of Ferocity, which somehow got printed despite depicting sexualized violence.  Like, really?  Aren’t these cards vetted?  Why did a variety of designers/developers/artists think it was okay to depict a man pinning a woman by her throat, raising a blade above her, with his knee between her thighs?  A full post about this here.

Avacyn Restored Pre-Release!

Apr 28th, 2012

My MTG clique decided to go to Green Lake Games at midnight for the AVR pre-release.  Woo!

(source)

I opened packs and was like, Okay, okay, red looks good…oh, here’s a Wolfir Silverheart (holographic!)…oh, Entreat the Angels, guess I’m also going white.  Sometimes I feel like Naya colors (red, green, and white) call to my soul (in the same way that Andou says white weenie is just “so me”), but I guess it doesn’t hurt that red/green together have AVR’s removal and bombs/fatties/enormous high-powered things.  In the end I went 3 and 1, crumpling at the end to another red/green player whose deck was way faster than mine, and had much better fat unblockable green support.

Final decklist:
Creatures
1x Angelic Wall
1x Nightshade Peddler
1x Kruin Striker
1x Falkenrath Exterminator
1x Hanweir Lancer
1x Fervent Cathar
1x Riot Ringleader
1x Mad Prophet
1x Goldnight Commander
1x Archwing Dragon
1x Wolfir Silverheart
1x Zealous Conscripts
1x Archangel

Spells
1x Pillar of Flame
1x Blessings of Nature
1x Thunderous Wrath
2x Cloudshift
1x Defang
1x Thunderbolt
1x Entreat the Angels
1x Cathar’s Crusade

Lands
7x Mountain
6x Plains
6x Forest

Notes:
  • After writing this list I wondered whether I should have just gone out full red/white (I guess I only had 3 green cards?!), but I did have a fun game with Wolfir Silverheart bonding with Kruin Striker for 8 points of trample damage…which definitely isn’t something that would have happened without green!
  • Archwing Dragon is SO GOOD.  The 4 mana cost turned out to be pretty “fast” against this set, where a lot of the heavier flyers were 7-mana angels.  Other flyers (like that Scrapskin Drake) can’t beat it, and the fact that it zips back into your hand at the end of every turn isn’t too bad/is pretty good, since it avoids things like Terminus.
  • Plus, it works so well with Goldnight Commander (the thing that says “Whenever another creature enters the battlefield under your control, creatures you control get +1/+1 until end of turn”).  A 5/5 swinging at your face?  Okay! :D
  • Or maybe it’s even a 6/6 because you Cloudshifted something else that was already on the field, as I did in one match when I also had an Archangel out.  Archwing as a 5/5 plus Archangel as a 7/7 with Vigilance?  Wooww
  • Cathar’s Crusade (“Whenever a creature enters the battlefield under your control, put a +1/+1 counter on each creature you control”) looked really fun, but in the end I could only shoot it off once, and aside from buffing Archwing Dragon slightly it didn’t really do much.  At other times I really just needed other things, like creatures.
  • Entreat the Angels: this last-minute unexpected “Miracle” thing sometimes feels like cheating, especially that one time when I can shot off 3 4/4 fliers for 5 mana
  • Angelic Wall: useless against the huge variety of green things that shut blockers down, like Champion of Lambholt or even Wandering Wolf (which is really easy in green to buff up to a 6/6 that’s unblockable and also avoids burn removal, ack).
  • At some point I had a Raging Poltergeist but it’s so disappointing to have to trade with Human tokens :( At some point I gave him First Strike with a Hanweir Lancer, but then the other guy gave his own guys First Strike, so, meh.

I really wanted to play with flicker effects for the pre-release, but just didn’t get the right pool for it.  Maybe I’ll get to during the actual release.

Pictures of Food Food Food Food

Apr 21st, 2012

truffles: lemon/lavender, yuzu/green tea, rosemary/salt, margarita
After I took this picture Andou asked, “Why do you suppose it’s such a ‘girl’ thing to take pictures of food?  Or like, do you remember that screenshot of the guy who asked how to get girls on Facebook, and all the girls were talking about how he should do something food-related and they would love him?”

We mulled over it and wondered if it might be because women and girls are inundated with “You must think about food!” in the same way boys and men are drenched in narratives that demand ”You must think about girls!”  Feeling sexy?  Food!  Feeling sad?  Food!  When famous women talk, they inevitably mention how they don’t diet (as Jennifer Lawrence did in last March’s Glamour), or else about how they do (as Lady Gaga did in a tweet), and people in society will reward and chastise them either way.  I’m not saying that men are never advertised to food-wise (clearly this is not the case), but I would be hard-pressed to say that men are encouraged to think about food the same way or magnitude women are.

Tiny Tower & Randomly Generated Diversity

Mar 30th, 2012

After failing some months ago to see what was so special about it, I gave Tiny Tower another try and began to see what was so entertaining about it.  I like that “leveling up” and the sense of progression is done via the number of floors you have in your tower, as well as the number of people that have been placed into their “dream jobs.”  Violet Perkins, for example, dresses like a pseudo-Viking and dreams of working in a bowling alley.

don’t worry, violet! i got your back!

The thing I like the most about Tiny Tower are these randomly generated people – specifically, their randomly generated complexions and genders and dreams.  It’s all random, but I can think of no other game with this variety of brown people, much less brown people with this variety of aspirations.

he dreams of browsing forums
while sugar-coating deep-fried dough

Amanda Garrett is non-white, non-male (potentially?), and wants to work in a Laboratory.  And she wears a hat!

GOSH YOU ARE SO AWESOME

Yeah, yeah – these little lives are admittedly shallow.  Amanda Garrett is just one combination of random numbers.  But, a character like Amanda Garrett appears rarely if ever in a video game.  And if she was, it would be pretty likely that her pixeled body would probably be contorted to fit the gamer gaze.

Lee Colman is another favorite bitizen of mine.  I have imagined him into a friendly, loud-voiced, outgoing dude with a hat he never removes, with an inexplicable gift for blending fruit into flavors that soothe the body and mind.  He halves and skins pineapples barehanded and closes up by faithfully polishing the blades of his processors, each a chef-caliber shuriken.


good ol’ lee colman

Now if only I didn’t feel so much like a druggie whenever I check my tower and make sure everything’s stocked.

Sakura & Magnolia

Mar 30th, 2012

Long ago, the then-Emperor of Japan gave the University of Washington a tribe of white (yoshino?) cherry trees. Each year they bloom, to the delight of DSLR-wielding students and inevitable caravans of bright-eyed people in wedding garments.


cherry trees in the quad

To be honest, this time of the year is a bit stressful for me.  The trees only bloom for a couple weeks, or less if rain shears off the blossoms.  Cherry blossoms remind me of all the mortality that my bittersweet chado class steeped into my heart.  This type of flower was apparently beloved of samurai and other short-lived warriors because, unlike other flowers, it falls at the fullest of its bloom.


death is near


When the cherry blossoms are blooming, all I can think is, I have to see them.  I have to take pictures of them!


EVEN IF IT’S WITH THIS GRAINY FAUX CIRCLE BLURRRR

This voracious desire does not quell until petals have congealed into pink gravel mud…at which point I almost want to breathe a sigh of relief.  When the flowers are blooming, I’m suddenly aware of all the minutes that pass by in which I’m not 100% enjoying the simple, ephemeral beauty of life.  With them gone, I can continue with life again, with the same wonderful ordinariness that weeks after Christmas/New Years have.

In past years I’ve spent this precious two weeks poking my lens right into the blobby clutches of flowers, but this year my camera is out of commission.  (I suspect/hope that it’s just the five-year-old battery that’s expended its last charge.)  This year, the couple times I’ve been able to steal onto campus, it’s always been dreary and damp.  The blossoms are plump with rainwater, the branches stooped with extra weight.

pretty gray out there

Without my camera, it’s hard to get a close-up view of the flowers and the water jeweled on them – but I suppose it gives the pictures I took this year a different flavor than last.  Given the opportunity, I’ll always turn the aperture up/down and get as close as possible to a single flower.  With only Instagram at my disposal and the noticeable not-wonderful quality of pictures from my iPod, I’m forced to step back and try to see a larger picture.


music hall tower


raitt hall

savery

miller

Also blooming at this time: magnolias! Did you know (according to Wikipedia) that magnolias existed before bees?  Though the blooms themselves are annually short-lived, it’s humbling to realize these flowers have been doing their thing for centuries before I was born, and will continue on blooming for centuries after the last time I’ll ever see them.
