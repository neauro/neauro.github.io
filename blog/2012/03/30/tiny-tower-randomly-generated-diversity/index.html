Tiny Tower & Randomly Generated Diversity

Mar 30th, 2012

After failing some months ago to see what was so special about it, I gave Tiny Tower another try and began to see what was so entertaining about it.  I like that “leveling up” and the sense of progression is done via the number of floors you have in your tower, as well as the number of people that have been placed into their “dream jobs.”  Violet Perkins, for example, dresses like a pseudo-Viking and dreams of working in a bowling alley.

don’t worry, violet! i got your back!

The thing I like the most about Tiny Tower are these randomly generated people – specifically, their randomly generated complexions and genders and dreams.  It’s all random, but I can think of no other game with this variety of brown people, much less brown people with this variety of aspirations.

he dreams of browsing forums
while sugar-coating deep-fried dough

Amanda Garrett is non-white, non-male (potentially?), and wants to work in a Laboratory.  And she wears a hat!

GOSH YOU ARE SO AWESOME

Yeah, yeah – these little lives are admittedly shallow.  Amanda Garrett is just one combination of random numbers.  But, a character like Amanda Garrett appears rarely if ever in a video game.  And if she was, it would be pretty likely that her pixeled body would probably be contorted to fit the gamer gaze.

Lee Colman is another favorite bitizen of mine.  I have imagined him into a friendly, loud-voiced, outgoing dude with a hat he never removes, with an inexplicable gift for blending fruit into flavors that soothe the body and mind.  He halves and skins pineapples barehanded and closes up by faithfully polishing the blades of his processors, each a chef-caliber shuriken.


good ol’ lee colman

Now if only I didn’t feel so much like a druggie whenever I check my tower and make sure everything’s stocked.
