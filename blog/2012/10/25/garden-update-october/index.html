Garden Update: October

Oct 25th, 2012

I have to admit that I was really pessimistic about those seeds, but they have been growing well! I even found someone growing seedlings in eggshells and decided to try this out too, leaving out some swiss chard seeds in potting mix on the back steps and covering them with some plastic to emulate the humidity that I hear encourages plants to sprout. And they are sprouting!

little things~

Unfortunately I wasn’t very scientific about my observations, so I don’t know if they sprouted faster than the chard planted straight into the ground. Oh well.

The “ground chard” is doing fine, by the way, though looking at this picture now I realize that the weeds around it are doing splendidly too.

gotta pull those, i guess

Also, the mystery plant that was sprouting last time has finally taken on a distinct shape and revealed itself to be spinach (I think)!

kinda looks like spinach, right?

Which means the other plant is definitely carrot.

yeah…definitely

The last thing we planted out back is cilantro, and it’s actually starting to look like cilantro!

whoa!

Now that the growing process was actually a success, I’m a little worried about how they’ll fare now autumn and its cool shroud has finally crept near. A coworker assures me that swiss chard is hardy as anything and that a friend of his had swiss chard faring crispily beneath even the snows of Snowpacalypse last year. For what it’s worth, I’ve been assembling high-tech cloches to shelter what plants I can from the chill and occasional hail that has precipitated recently.

do yo thing, my pretties

As for the front yard, that agastache is blooming, though there are no hummingbirds in sight. Maybe next year.

you are so hard to take a picture of

Herb alley is okay…

and fragrant as ever

And some of my sedums were, unfortunately, starting to look not-that-great, so I transplanted them into a ridiculously large planter where I hope they’ll have more room to grow. When I was moving them around I was a dismayed to see the Miracle Gro potting soil was densely packed and damp around the plants that had never grown that strongly (in particular, the plant second from the right and last on the left). So, no more Miracle Gro I guess. This potting soil is from Carpinito Brothers and the sedums seem okay.

shrug

 Last but not least, my air plants, whose pups are growing well if slowly. Actually, I was surprised to see that the stricta (pictured left) started developing a second pup off to the side!

you go, girl
